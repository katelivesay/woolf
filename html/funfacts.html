Fun Facts about Woolf

1. Virginia Woolf's birth name was Adeline Virginia Stephen. She began using her married name, Woolf, after her marriage to Leonard Woolf in 1912.

2. Woolf was known for her love of pets, particularly dogs. She had several throughout her life, including a spaniel named Pinka who appears in her novel "Flush."

3. Woolf was a member of the Bloomsbury Group, a circle of writers, artists, and intellectuals who were known for their unconventional lifestyles and progressive views. Other members of the group included E.M. Forster, Lytton Strachey, and Woolf's sister, Vanessa Bell.

4. Woolf was a talented painter and studied art at the Slade School of Fine Art in London. Some of her paintings are now on display at the Tate Britain museum in London.

5. Woolf was an avid reader and often read several books at once. She also kept a detailed diary throughout her life, which has been published in multiple volumes.

6. Woolf's novel "Mrs. Dalloway" was inspired by her own experiences of walking through London and observing the people and events around her.

7.Woolf was a vocal feminist and wrote several essays on the topic, including "A Room of One's Own," which argues for women's financial independence in order to achieve creative freedom.

8. Woolf struggled with mental illness throughout her life and was diagnosed with bipolar disorder. She received various treatments, including psychotherapy and medication, but ultimately took her own life in 1941.

9. Woolf's most famous work, "To the Lighthouse," is based on her childhood memories of summers spent in St. Ives, Cornwall, with her family.

10. Woolf was a fan of cricket and enjoyed watching matches with her husband, Leonard. In fact, she once wrote that "a good innings" was one of the few things that could cheer her up when she was feeling down.
